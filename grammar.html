Universum Grammar

Sentence Structure

Basic Word Order: Subject-Verb-Object (SVO).

  • Ya benu soru. (I eat food.)
  • Ye tuki fali. (You see a tree.)

Questions

Formed by adding "na" at the beginning.

  • Na ya benu soru? (Do I eat food?)
  • Na ye tuki fali? (Do you see a tree?)

Negation

Utilizes the particle "ne" placed before the verb.

  • Ya ne benu soru. (I do not eat food.)
  • Ye ne tuki fali. (You do not see a tree.)

Tense and Aspect

Marked by particles "ta" (past), "te" (present), "to" (future) before the verb.

  • Ya ta benu soru. (I ate food.)
  • Ye to tuki fali. (You will see a tree.)

Plurality

Indicated by the particle "ku" placed after the noun. Singular forms do not use a marker unless clarity is needed.

  • Fali ku lata pika. (Trees move fast.)
  • Soru ku benu. (Eat foods.)

Pronouns

Pronouns do not change form and are not gendered.

  • Ya pika. (I go.)
  • Yo benu soru ku. (We eat foods.)

Possession

Expressed with possessive pronouns.

  • Yasa soru. (My food.)
  • YoSa batofali. (Our house.)

Adjectives and Adverbs

Placed directly before the nouns or verbs they modify without changing form.

  • Puno fali pika lata. (The big tree moves fast.)
  • Mifi soru benu lata. (Eat the soft food quickly.)

Comparison

Made with the particles "mo" (more) and "mos" (most) before the adjective or adverb.

  • Mo puno fali pika lata. (The bigger tree moves fast.)
  • Mos mifi soru benu lata. (Eat the softest food quickly.)

Prepositions

Precede the noun phrase they modify.

  • Ya pika ba batofali. (I go to the house.)
  • Ya benu soru be lumi. (I eat food under the air/sky.)

Conjunctions

Connect clauses or phrases without changing form.

  • Ya benu soru ke pika ba batofali. (I eat food and go to the house.)
  • Ya tuki fali ko tuki rino. (I see a tree or see an animal.)

Imperatives

Commands are formed using the verb without the subject. The tense particle is omitted for immediate commands.

  • Benu soru! (Eat food!)
  • Pika ba batofali! (Go to the house!)

Interjections

Express emotions or reactions and can be used standalone or at the beginning of sentences.

  • Wa! Ya tuki puno fali. (Wow! I see a big tree.)
  • TaLe, ye benu soru. (Thank you, you eat food.)
