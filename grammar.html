Grammar

Learn how to form sentences in Universum.

Sentence Structure

The language follows a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) structure for sentences. Questions use a particle 'Fa' at the beginning, and negation is marked by 'ne' before the verb.

Tense and Aspect

Tenses are marked by particles 'ta' for past, 'te' for present, and 'to' for future, placed before the verb.

Nouns and Pronouns

Plurality is indicated by 'ka' after the noun. Pronouns are straightforward and do not change form based on gender.

Adjectives

Adjectives are placed directly before the nouns they modify. Comparative and superlative forms use the particles 'ma' and 'mos' respectively.

Prepositions and Conjunctions

Prepositions precede the noun phrases they modify. Basic conjunctions like 'ke' (and), 'ko' (or), and 'ku' (but) connect clauses simply.

Commands and Interjections

Commands omit the subject and tense particle for immediacy. Interjections can express emotions or reactions and are used standalone or at the beginning of sentences.
