Universum Phonology

Consonants

Universum features a balanced set of consonants, including plosives, nasals, fricatives, and approximants/liquids. Here are examples of words using these consonants:

  • pali (water) - Example of a plosive
  • nifi (six) - Example of a nasal
  • feko (old) - Example of a fricative
  • lumi (air) - Example of an approximant/liquid

Vowels

The vowel system in Universum is designed to be maximally distinct with front, back, and no central vowels:

  • tane (earth) - Example of a front vowel /e/
  • kuna (four) - Example of a back vowel /u/

Syllable Structure

Every syllable in Universum follows a consonant-vowel-consonant-vowel (CVCV) pattern, promoting clarity and ease of pronunciation. Examples include:

  • bato (stone)
  • soru (food)

Phonotactic Constraints

Universum avoids consonant clusters and encourages natural vowel harmony. Examples showcasing this feature:

  • menu (person) - No consonant clusters
  • lana (twelve) - Natural vowel harmony

Stress Pattern

Stress in Universum words is predictably fixed on the first syllable, aiding in word recognition and pronunciation. Examples:

  • 'menu (person)
  • 'bato (stone)

Note: The apostrophe (') indicates stress.
