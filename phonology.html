Phonology

Learn how to speak in Universum.

Consonants and Vowels

The consonant inventory of Universum includes plosives like /p/, /t/, /b/, /k/, nasals /m/, /n/, fricatives /f/, /v/, /s/, and liquids /l/, /r/. The vowels are simple and distinct with /i/, /e/, /a/, /o/, /u/.

Syllable Structure

Each syllable in Universum follows a CVCV pattern. This structure avoids complex consonant clusters, ensuring each consonant and vowel is pronounced clearly, which facilitates learning and pronunciation.

Phonotactic Constraints

Universum does not allow consonant clusters and maintains a simple vowel system that encourages natural vowel harmony. Stress is fixed on the first syllable, simplifying the rhythm of speech.

Prosodic Features

Pitch and intonation in Universum use a rising intonation for questions and falling intonation for statements. The language adopts a syllable-timed rhythm, contributing to its clarity and ease of pronunciation.
